A group can be an organization, school, recreational business, league, team, family... the possibilities are endless! At any time, you can add groups below or above your group. For example, an organization might have leagues made up of teams. Each organization, league and team is a separate group. The league has a group above (the organization) and groups below (the teams.)

To create a new group, log in to your account or create a new account.

Then, in the navigation bar, go to Group > Create a Group or click the Create group button in the Groups box.


If you don't have an AllPlayers.com account yet, follow the instructions here to sign up.

Enter basic group information

  • Group Name: The name of your organization. Your group page URL will be automatically created based on the name of your group.
  • Search Terms: Displayed to visitors searching for activities.

If you are creating a team, we suggest adding your main Group Above in the Search Terms field to help visitors find you. For example, if your Group Name is Eagles (the name of your team), and your team is part of the Springfield Rec Center Winter 2011 Basketball League, include Springfield Rec Center Winter 2011 Basketball League in this field.


  • Location: Your main address. Only the ZIP code field is required; however, if your group has a physical location, we recommend entering it to help your members find you.

  • Category Info: Choose the category that best describes your group. This will help customize your group page with information relevant to your members. Site visitors can also search for groups by category.

Configure settings

  • Active: Activates the group when you complete the Create Group form.
  • Fee (Approval Required): Submits a request for approval to accept online registration payments for your group. If you would like your group members to be able to register and pay for your group through AllPlayers.com, turn this ON. Your request will automatically be sent to membership@allplayers.com.
  • Group Mates: Allows members of your group to have a relationship with one another, so they can see each other's profiles and send messages to one another while the group is active.
  • Groups Above: Used to establish a relationship between groups. For example, if you are creating groups that are teams, and these teams belong to a league, the league is the group above the team.

Configure features

Auto Schedule Events: Allows you to automatically create league game schedules, based on teams and resources you assign to the group.

Calendar: Allows you to create and post events to your group. Your group page will display a calendar, and your events will automatically populate in your members' personal calendars. TIP: You should enable Calendar if you plan to use the Auto Schedule Events feature.

Downloads: Allows you to easily add downloadable files, such as PDF forms or files you need to distribute to your members, to your group pages.

Group Roster: Allows you to view and edit subgroup rosters.

Photos: Allows you to create photo albums and upload photos to your group pages.

Roster: Allows group administrators to manage the roster for your group.

Sports: Provides scores, schedules, and standings.

Tournament Brackets: Allows you to create a wide variety of tournament brackets, enter scores and automatically advance teams through a tournament.

Additional Options

The last step to create your group is to configure the colors and logo for your group. Choose two colors that complement your group's colors, and upload a logo or image for your group.

You're Done!

Click Create Group when you're ready, and you'll be taken directly to your new group home page.
