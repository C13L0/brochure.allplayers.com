In our example, we have three subgroups of Springfield Recreation Center. These are the programs that our sample recreation center offers: Camp, Sports, and After School.

We'll create the Sports group next.

For this demonstration, we'll start from our sample group page for Springfield Recreation Center. It's simple to create the Sports group as a subgroup of our main entity.

In this guide, we'll use our sample organizational chart for the fictional Springfield Recreation Center to demonstrate how you can easily create groups.

If your organization is new to AllPlayers.com, create your top group first.

×

If you need to turn an existing group into a Subgroup of another group, you can do so through the Group Settings. Instructions follow the tutorial below; scroll down to Assign the Group Above to a new or existing group

Create a subgroup of an existing group

From the front page that will be the Group Above your new group, go to More Tools in your Group Toolbox and choose Subgroup.

Complete the form with your subgroup information.

For full details on creating a group, see Create your group and Configure settings and features.

×

Your Group Name should differentiate your groups in your hierarchy. Your Search Terms will display when visitors search to Find Groups. We recommend including your topmost Group Above as part of your Search Terms to help identify your group in search results, since a large number of organizations could have a group named "Winter Basketball" or a team named "Eagles."

When you have finished and clicked Create Group, you'll be returned to your group page.

You can find your subgroup under your Groups menu at the top of the page.

To see the Subgroups for your current group, open your Dashboard, choose Subgroups from the Add dropdown, and drag the Subgroups: Active box onto your page.

You can also show a hierarchal tree of your Groups Below by dragging the Groups Below: Tree View box onto your page. See Show organization hierarchy tree for more information.

Assign a group above to a new existing group.

When you create a new group, you may select a Group Above in the Settings section of the form.

For existing groups, select More Tools > Group Settings in your Group Toolbox. Scroll down to Groups Above and choose the group above your current group.
